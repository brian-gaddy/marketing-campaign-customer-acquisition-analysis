# Problem Statement

## Scenario

Marketing mix is a widely used concept in the execution of marketing strategies. It includes several areas of a marketing plan and is often organized around the Four Ps of Marketing: product, price, place, and promotion.

## Objective

As a data scientist, the objective is to conduct exploratory data analysis and hypothesis testing to better understand the factors that influence customer acquisition and campaign response.

## Data Context

The dataset aligns with the Four Ps of Marketing:

- **Product:** Spending across categories such as wine, fruit, meat, fish, sweets, and gold products.
- **Price:** Income and deal-based purchases that indicate purchasing power and price sensitivity.
- **Place:** Shopping channels such as web, catalog, and store purchases.
- **Promotion:** Campaign acceptance, complaints, recency, and customer engagement variables.
- **Demographics:** Education, marital status, age, children at home, and country.

## Analytical Tasks

The project includes the following analytical tasks:

1. Validate imported variables such as customer enrollment date and income.
2. Clean education and marital-status categories.
3. Impute missing income values using comparable customer groups.
4. Create variables for total children, age, total spending, and total purchases.
5. Use box plots and histograms to review distributions and outliers.
6. Apply ordinal and one-hot encoding where appropriate.
7. Build a correlation heatmap.
8. Test hypotheses related to age, children, sales channels, and geography.
9. Visualize product revenue, campaign response, country performance, household behavior, and customer complaints.
